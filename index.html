    📦 Box Breathing - Step by Step

    Step 1: Find Your Comfort Zone

    Sit upright in a comfortable position with good posture. You can sit in a chair, cross-legged on the floor, or on a meditation cushion. Make sure your spine is straight but not tense. Keep your shoulders relaxed.

    Step 2: Empty Your Lungs

    Take a moment to notice your breath. Then slowly exhale completely through your mouth, emptying all the air from your lungs. Don't force it - let it be natural and gentle.

    Step 3: First Side - Inhale for 4 Counts

    Close your mouth and inhale slowly through your nose while counting to 4 in your head (1-2-3-4). Make it smooth and controlled, not rushed. You're now on the first side of the box.

    Step 4: Second Side - Hold for 4 Counts

    After reaching the count of 4, hold your breath gently without any tension. Count to 4 (1-2-3-4) while keeping the air in your lungs. This is the second side of the box.

    Step 5: Third Side - Exhale for 4 Counts

    Now slowly exhale through your mouth or nose for 4 counts (1-2-3-4). Make it smooth and complete, releasing all the air. This completes the third side of the box.

    Step 6: Fourth Side - Hold Empty for 4 Counts

    Your lungs are now empty. Hold this empty state for 4 counts (1-2-3-4) without tension. Then repeat the cycle. Continue for 5-10 repetitions or until you feel calm.

    ✨ Benefits:

    ✅ Reduces anxiety and stress
    ✅ Improves focus and concentration
    ✅ Calms the nervous system
    ✅ Easy to learn and practice
    ✅ Can be done anytime, anywhere

    😴 4-7-8 Relaxation Breathing - The Sleep Technique

    Step 1: Prepare Your Position

    Sit upright with your back comfortably against a chair or wall. Relax your shoulders completely. You can also do this lying in bed before sleep. Let your entire body settle into a relaxed state.

    Step 2: Open Your Mouth & Exhale

    Open your mouth slightly and exhale completely through your mouth with a whoosh sound. Release all the stale air from your lungs. This is a cleansing breath to prepare your system.

    Step 3: Nose Inhale for 4 Counts

    Close your mouth. Inhale quietly through your nose while counting to 4 (1-2-3-4). Make it slow and controlled. Feel your belly expand as you breathe deeply.

    Step 4: Hold Your Breath for 7 Counts

    Close your mouth and nostrils. Hold your breath gently for 7 counts (1-2-3-4-5-6-7). This longer hold is key to activating the parasympathetic nervous system. Don't force it.

    Step 5: Exhale for 8 Counts

    Open your mouth and exhale completely for 8 counts (1-2-3-4-5-6-7-8). Make it a long, slow exhale. The long exhale is what triggers deep relaxation and sleep.

    Step 6: Repeat 4 Cycles

    Complete the cycle 4 times. That's: inhale (4), hold (7), exhale (8). Repeat this 4 times. For sleep, practice this as you lie in bed. For daily anxiety, practice 2x per day.

    ✨ Benefits:

    ✅ Promotes deep sleep
    ✅ Reduces anxiety
    ✅ Calms racing thoughts
    ✅ Lowers heart rate
    ✅ Perfect for insomnia

    🫁 Diaphragmatic Breathing - Belly Breathing

    Step 1: Choose Your Position

    You can lie on your back on a yoga mat or bed, or sit in a comfortable chair. If lying down, place a pillow under your knees for support. Keep your arms at your sides or on your belly.

    Step 2: Hand Placement for Awareness

    Place one hand on your chest and one hand on your belly. This helps you feel the difference between shallow chest breathing and deep belly breathing. You want the hand on your belly to move more.

    Step 3: Belly Expands on Inhale

    Breathe in slowly through your nose. Focus on making your belly expand (not your chest). Imagine you're breathing all the way down to your belly button. The hand on your belly should rise.

    Step 4: Slow, Complete Exhale

    Exhale slowly through your mouth or nose. Feel your belly naturally fall back down. Make the exhale about as long or longer than the inhale. This triggers the relaxation response.

    Step 5: Continue Naturally

    Keep breathing at a natural pace, belly expanding and contracting. After a few minutes, you can remove your hands and just feel the rhythm naturally.

    Step 6: Practice 5-15 Minutes Daily

    Continue this pattern for 5-15 minutes. Practice daily, ideally in the morning or before bed. With practice, this becomes your natural breathing pattern.

    ✨ Benefits:

    ✅ Activates the relaxation response
    ✅ Lowers blood pressure
    ✅ Increases oxygen intake
    ✅ Reduces stress dramatically
    ✅ Improves digestion

    👃 Alternate Nostril Breathing (Nadi Shodhana)

    Step 1: Sit in a Comfortable Position

    Sit upright on a cushion or chair with your spine straight. Relax your shoulders and rest your hands on your lap or knees. You can also sit cross-legged if comfortable. Take a few deep breaths to settle yourself.

    Step 2: Position Your Hand (Vishnu Mudra)

    Raise your right hand. Fold your index and middle fingers toward your palm. Keep your ring finger, pinky finger, and thumb extended. This is called Vishnu Mudra. This hand position helps you control each nostril.

    Step 3: Close Your Right Nostril

    Place your right thumb on your right nostril and close it gently. Don't press hard - just a gentle closure is enough. Now you're ready to breathe through your left nostril only.

    Step 4: Inhale Through Left Nostril

    Close your right nostril with your thumb. Inhale slowly and deeply through your left nostril to the count of 4. Make the breath smooth and controlled. Feel the cool air entering your left nostril.

    Step 5: Close Both Nostrils & Hold

    At the top of your inhale, use your ring finger or pinky to close your left nostril as well. Now both nostrils are closed. Hold your breath gently for a count of 4. This is a critical point - don't force it.

    Step 6: Exhale Through Right Nostril

    Release your right nostril (remove your thumb) while keeping the left nostril closed. Exhale slowly and completely through your right nostril to the count of 4. Feel the warm air leaving through your right side.

    Step 7: Reverse the Pattern

    Now inhale through your right nostril (count of 4), hold both nostrils closed (count of 4), then exhale through your left nostril (count of 4). This completes one full cycle of alternate nostril breathing.

    Step 8: Repeat 5-10 Cycles

    Continue this pattern for 5-10 complete cycles. Start slowly and steadily. As you practice, you may increase to 8-10 count cycles or add a longer hold period. Practice daily for best results - ideally in the morning.

    ✨ Benefits:

    ✅ Balances left and right brain hemispheres
    ✅ Improves focus and mental clarity
    ✅ Calms anxiety and nervousness
    ✅ Clears nasal passages
    ✅ Enhances overall energy

    🔨 Bellows Breath (Bhastrika) - The Energizer

    Step 1: Warm-Up with Box Breathing

    Before doing bellows breath, do a few rounds of Box Breathing (4-4-4-4) to prepare your respiratory system. This warm-up helps you practice safely and effectively.

    Step 2: Sit Upright with Good Posture

    Sit in a comfortable upright position - either cross-legged on the floor or on a chair. Keep your spine straight. This allows maximum lung expansion. Keep your shoulders relaxed and your chest open.

    Step 3: Take a Deep Preliminary Breath

    Take one full, deep breath in through your nose and exhale completely through your mouth. This prepares your lungs for the rapid breathing pattern that follows. Feel your entire respiratory system activate.

    Step 4: Rapid Inhales Through Nose

    Now begin rapid, forceful inhales through your nose. Each inhale should be quick and powerful - like a bellows pumping air. Your abdomen will expand and contract rapidly. Do about 10-20 rapid inhales to start. Let the rhythm build.

    Step 5: Rapid Exhales Through Mouth

    Each rapid inhale is matched by a rapid exhale through your mouth. The breathing should sound like a strong wind or bellows. Inhale-exhale, inhale-exhale, rapid and powerful. Feel the energy building in your body.

    Step 6: Complete Your Cycles

    Do 1-3 rounds of 20 rapid breaths. After each round, stop and breathe normally. Between rounds, take 5-10 normal breaths to recover. Beginners should do 1 round; experienced practitioners can do 2-3 rounds.

    Step 7: Rest and Feel the Energy

    After all rounds are complete, sit quietly and observe the sensations in your body. You may feel tingling, warmth, or a surge of energy. This is completely normal. Rest for 1-2 minutes before resuming normal activities.

    ⚠️ Important Safety Notes:

    🔹 Start with just 1 round of 10 breaths if you're new
    🔹 Do NOT practice if you have high blood pressure or heart conditions
    🔹 Avoid if pregnant
    🔹 Best done on an empty stomach
    🔹 Stop immediately if you feel dizzy

    ✨ Benefits:

    ✅ Boosts energy and vitality
    ✅ Increases mental alertness
    ✅ Clears mental fog
    ✅ Improves circulation
    ✅ Perfect for morning practice

    🦁 Lion's Breath (Simhasana) - Release Your Roar

    Step 1: Get Into Lion's Pose Position

    Kneel on the floor or sit on a chair. Keep your back straight. If kneeling, sit back on your heels. Place your hands on your knees, palms down. Keep your feet pointed back (if kneeling) or flat on the ground (if sitting).

    Step 2: Take a Deep Breath In

    Inhale deeply and completely through your nose. Fill your lungs with air. Feel your chest expand. Your abdomen should rise as your lungs fill. Prepare yourself for a powerful exhale.

    Step 3: Open Your Mouth Wide

    Open your mouth as wide as possible, like a lion roaring. Stretch your jaw open fully. This is key to the practice - the wider the better. Feel the stretch in your jaw and face.

    Step 4: Stick Out Your Tongue

    Extend your tongue out and downward as far as possible. Let it point toward your chin. The tongue should be fully relaxed and stretched. Don't tense it - just let it extend naturally.

    Step 5: Exhale Forcefully with Sound

    Exhale the air out forcefully through your mouth with a loud "HA" sound. Make it powerful! This is like a lion's roar. The sound should come from deep in your belly. Pour all the air out completely in one strong exhale.

    Step 6: Relax and Return to Normal

    Close your mouth and return your tongue to normal position. Take a few natural, gentle breaths to recover. Notice how your face, jaw, and entire body feels. You may feel a sense of release or lightness.

    Step 7: Repeat 3-5 Times

    Repeat the entire sequence (steps 2-6) a total of 3-5 times. Each time, put your full energy and intention into the roar. Feel yourself releasing tension and stress with each lion's breath.

    Step 8: Observe the Release

    After completing your repetitions, sit quietly and notice the effects. Many people feel tension released from their face, jaw, neck, and shoulders. You may feel more relaxed, lighter, or energized. This is the power of deliberate tension release.

    💡 Variations:

    🔹 Angry Lion: Contract your facial muscles while doing the breath
    🔹 Playful Lion: Keep the expression soft and playful
    🔹 Silent Lion: Do the same movements but without the "HA" sound

    ✨ Benefits:

    ✅ Releases tension from face and jaw
    ✅ Reduces headaches
    ✅ Improves facial circulation
    ✅ Builds confidence and empowerment
    ✅ Relieves stress and anxiety
    ✅ Excellent for TMJ relief
